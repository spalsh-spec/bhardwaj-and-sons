## Notability — the actual constraint Wikipedia will enforce

Wikipedia's company-notability standard (**WP:CORP / WP:NCORP**) requires:

1. **Significant coverage** — multiple independent reliable secondary sources covering the company *in depth* (not passing mentions, not press-release rewrites)
2. **Independence** — the sources must be *independent* of the company (not blogs by the founder, not interviews where the founder is the only voice, not press releases distributed by the founder)
3. **Reliable** — established journalism, peer-reviewed papers, books published by reputable presses, etc.
4. **Secondary** — analysis or commentary, not the company's own self-published material

For a 2-month-old single-founder Australian house, **none of these are met today.** What needs to happen first:

| Source needed | How to earn it |
|---|---|
| **3+ news articles** | Get coverage from *The Australian*, *AFR*, *Information Week*, *MIT Tech Review*, *Wired*, *VentureBeat*, etc. Typically follows a high-impact paper or product launch |
| **Academic citations** | Get the methodology paper into arXiv, then cited by 3+ independent papers (~12-18 months) |
| **Industry analyst coverage** | Gartner / Forrester reports mentioning the company. Typically requires established commercial revenue |
| **Major customer announcement** | An Anthropic / Google / regulatory body publicly announcing they use the methodology. Hardest to engineer; happens when the work is good enough |

**Realistic submission window: 12-24 months from now.** Sooner if a major commercial deal lands or HN/arXiv launch goes viral.
